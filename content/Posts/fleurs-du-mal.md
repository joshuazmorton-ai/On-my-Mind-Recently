“Love and hate share a stem”  
she said, knowing love is an un-hemmed  
thing, with string sticking from every edge  
getting caught alongside every ledge.   

A knit so easy to fall apart  
as the string rips against another’s heart.  
A dart through the night sky  
made me wish I could fly,  
high above where my love would never be caught.  

But wings aren’t something to be bought,  
and people only have the love that was brought  
with them at the moment of birth.  
All love does not grow or shrink on the earth.  

So the roots of love feed the stems of hate,  
and both are served on the same plate.  
There's no bait to catch love, it's something grown.  
There's no preparation, it's never something known.  

I know nothing, maybe I will in time.  
Love and hate are one flower, the stem a thin line.
