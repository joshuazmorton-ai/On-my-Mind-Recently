Light brown eyes that  
somehow reflect an orange sunset  
better than any mirror.  
Yet they say blue is the clearest colour.  

Falling for her  
under cloudy January skies  
A light grey twinkled with specs of sky.  
Yet they say blue is the clearest colour.  

The green of the grass by the lake  
we walked passed, walking you home  
in early March.  
Yellow sun finally gracing warmth upon our skin,  
twinkling again off her eyes.  
Yet they say blue is the clearest colour.  

The green of love,  
a blooming huisache tree.  
Vibrant golden glances on the buds,  
green growing leaves on every branch —  
a bit of life for every situation.  
Yet they say blue is the clearest colour.  

The fallout.  
The dark gloomy blue.  
The murky teal droplets corroding my stomach,  
the light blue water from my eyes.  
And I know blue is the clearest colour.
