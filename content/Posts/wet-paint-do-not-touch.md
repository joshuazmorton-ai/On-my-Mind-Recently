“Wet paint do not touch,”  
but the glossy shine called to me.  
Deep complex colour, i could  
use it, feel it.  
I could create new beauties, new art.  
I see so much in wet paint.  

But the sign says “do not touch.”  

If i touched the wet paint,  
Even just for a small faint  
moment, what would come?  
My fingerprint forever dried on,  
an imprint, a scar.  

So the sign says “do not touch”  

It’s not complete, the coatings of pigment blood  
Smeared on a healing surface.  
Like me, incomplete; untouchable.  
Yet as I dry, I am touched by many.  

But as many touch wet paint,  
am I destined to peel and destroy?
