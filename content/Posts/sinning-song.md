Am I born good?\
I’ve grown to know to do the things I should.\
Am I moral in intention?\
I want what’s good, but so often act in contention.

The things I want are not sin,\
but it's ingrained so deep within.\
To cure it, cut it out, a surgery.\
Sin was an inheritance, a man who always stood by me. 

Perhaps I am instinct as an animal.\
Evil eats me, yet it is a cannibal.\
I am the second coming of the great god Paan.\
It isn’t right to blame my nature, but I am just a mere man.

I try to be stoic, stay away,\
til the belt upon Brutus leads me astray.\
The shame alienates my heart and name.\
The Guilt to trap me in his cyclic game. 

Someone help me come to my senses,\
I am weak and I am defenseless.\
I am stuck behind gray fences,\
and my goodness feels like pretenses. 

Evil one, it’s hard; I want you gone\
but we’re the same, and I’d miss me.\
You throw rocks down at my window.\
You’re the worst but all I know. 

I am a dog returning to vomit.\
I am a beast behind a sonnet.\
I’ve never kept away for long,\
I bury faults inside my song.\
Ain’t it funny freedom makes you a prisoner.\
Ain’t it funny I can’t help but be a sinner.
