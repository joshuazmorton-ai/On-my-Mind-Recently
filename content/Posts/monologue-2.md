Bright green leaves in the sun,  
a stubborn stump refusing to run.  
Slowly inching with bark back facing skies,  
it hunches over a river — nearly sideways it lies.  
    
Heaven struck the leaning tree  
and lumber sank with a third degree.  
Broken scorned stump alone remained,  
and despite small and drained,  
only looked up to climb.  
It shot straight up as a pine.  
Thicker and higher the tree did shine,  
no fear of falling, unworthy to whine.  
    
Even though he was cut again for paper,  
they turned him into a book on nature.  
It told of trees reaching to the heavens as if they vowed  
to reach the sun and thank the clouds.  
    
Broken things can come back greater  
and in time I will rise like trees – up to my creator.  
But until then, I soak up sun  
and grow to become a better son.
